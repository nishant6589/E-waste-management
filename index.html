About Us

The main goal of this project is to reduce the E-Waste present in our surrounding. We know that the decomposition of a E-waste take a long period of time. We are going to implement another form of way by using the RRR - Reduce, Reuse, Recycle methods. So ,we wanated to construct a linkage system between electronic waste generators and electronic waste management system. The waste generated can be directly handled by the management companies or plant. These also helps in segregation of the materials after pre-processing the instruments.

What we do with the e-waste

High Pressure Moulding:

In this method the materials like printed circuit boards (pcbs), motherboards etc are collected and are melted down , during the process some materials like aluminium , nickel etc are add to it to increase the properties of the materials .

Repair of gadgets:

A repair would be better than a replacement for the following situations: There is no storage left. The owner is starting to have a hard time using the device. The screen is cracked. The owner wants an upgrade just for the sake of it.

Upcycling

Upcycling is not the same as recycling. “Recycling takes consumer materials — mostly plastic, paper, metal and glass — and breaks them down so their base materials can be remade into a new consumer product, often of lesser quality,” When you upcycle an item, you aren’t breaking down the materials.

Batteries Recycling:

There are a lot of good reasons to recycle your used electronics. Lithium-ion batteries in e-Waste is one of them .Some of these materials are toxic. We want to keep those toxins out of landfills, where they can contaminate the soil and water .

Green Electronics:

Green electronics refer to electronics that are biodegradable, sustainable, and recyclable.When we usually think of biodegradable, we think of banana peels or apples; something that breaks down and disappears after a few days. But, no one wants an iPhone that turns into mush in your pocket .Green electronics, though, are durable during use yet made to biodegrade at end-of-life.
